PDD

Delusional Disorder

Persistent Delusional Disorder — ICD-11 term

History Pearls

  • PC: most of the time admitted by family members & police
  • Describe the episode — what happened, may be an attempt of aggression
  • Try to elicit the delusion — 100% conviction is important, otherwise it is an overvalued idea
  • Checking behaviour, frequent arguments, attempt of confrontation of wife / harming alleged lover
  • Exclude 1st rank symptoms, manic/depressive episodes, anxiety disorders, obsessional thoughts
  • Types: infidelity (most common), persecutory, D. of love, somatic, grandiose, armed
  • Other forms of morbid jealousy: overvalued idea, obsessional thought
  • Personal hx: previous relationships & why they broke up; alcohol/substance dependence, last intake
  • Pre-morbid personality: suspiciousness, difficulty trusting people, priority-seeking, insecurity
  • Collateral hx from partner — any violence, physical / sexual abuse

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Presence of a delusion or set of related delusions
  • Persisting for at least 3 months (≥3 months)
  • Remarkable stability within individuals
  • Absence of depressive, manic or mixed episode
  • Absence of schizophrenia symptoms — clear & persistent hallucinations, severely disorganized thinking, influence/passivity/control, negative symptoms (specific hallucinations/illusions related to delusion content may be present)
  • Affect, speech & behaviour typically unaffected
  • Not a manifestation of another medical condition / substance / medication

MSE & Risk

  • A & B — if no depression, self care is good
  • Risk to self: self harm, suicidal, physical health
  • Risk to others: violence to children, partner violence, physical/sexual abuse

Management

Acute

Biological

  • Decide setting
  • If aggressive → Mx
  • Ensure safe environment
  • O. Risperidone 2mg n (Max 16mg)
  • Olanzapine 5mg n (Max 20mg)
  • Before starting: FBS, Lipid profile
  • O. Clonazepam 0.5mg n short duration if poor sleep
  • Mx withdrawal during ward stay if alcohol dependence

DEM

Dementia

Cognitive assessment, BPSD, MMSE & Alzheimer's management

History Pearls

  • PC may be due to BPSD, or due to progressive memory loss
  • If due to BPSD, explain progression, symptoms & likely precipitating events
  • BPSD behavioural: physical aggression, screaming, restlessness, wandering, culturally inappropriate behaviour, sexual disinhibition, hoarding, cursing
  • BPSD psychological: anxiety, depressive mood, apathy, hallucinations & delusions, elation, negativism
  • Precipitants: unmet needs (hunger/thirst/pain), over/under-stimulation, acute medical problems, life stressors, hearing/vision impairment, communication issues
  • Assess each cognitive domain: memory (long/short/semantic), language (word finding, naming, reading/writing), praxis (dressing, brushing teeth), abstract thinking & judgement (money, decisions), visuoperceptual skills (getting lost, recognizing faces), personality & social conduct (withdrawal, disinhibition)
  • Who is caregiving? Impact to family members & caregiver

Diagnostic Criteria

  • DD exclusion — Delirium: fever, fluctuating consciousness, acute onset
  • Brain tumours: chronic headache, vision impairment, seizures
  • Hypothyroidism: cold intolerance, fatigue, constipation
  • Vit B12 ↓: tingling & numbness of peripheries
  • Vascular dementia: hx of strokes, body weakness
  • Parkinsonism: slowing of movement, gait abnormalities
  • Frontotemporal: early behavioural change (disinhibition), no early memory/personality change
  • Exclude depression as well (does low mood or memory loss come first?)

MSE & Risk

  • A & B: facial expressions, hand gestures, tremors, gait abnormalities
  • MMSE — 11 components, total 30: Time(5), Place(5), Serial 7s(5), Registration(3), Recall(3), Naming(2), 3-stage command(3), Reading(1), Writing(1), Repetition(1), Pentagon(1)
  • Scoring: ≥24 normal (easy rule); 23–20 mild; 19–10 moderate; <10 severe
  • Risk: self negligence & poor self care, accidents, getting lost, self harm/suicidal
  • To others: violence. From others: financial / physical exploitation
  • Physical exam: hyper/hypothyroid signs, Parkinson's signs — resting tremor, cog-wheel / lead-pipe rigidity, festinating gait

Management

Acute

Biological

  • Rx setting — most of the time no need to admit; admit only if aggressive or significant risk
  • 1. Go through past records
  • 2. Investigate for reversible causes of cognitive impairment
  • FBC (macrocytic anaemia, B9/B12↓)
  • ESR (autoimmune cerebral involvement)
  • S.Cr, SE (renal failure)
  • Liver biochem (liver failure)
  • TFTs (hypothyroidism)
  • NCCT brain (structural lesions)
  • CRP, UPR (if suspect delirium)

Psychological

  • Objective cognitive assessment — MMSE / MOCA
  • Psychoeducation to family & pt: nature of illness, not curable, importance of caregiver, pharm/non-pharm options
  • Advice on home care: good lighting/clock/calendar, night light, grab rails, walking stick, reminisce with albums, fulfil basic needs, lock doors at night
  • Behavioural symptoms can also be addressed with above measures

Social

  • Assessment of caregiver burden — sharing responsibility, screen for depression
  • Help solve accommodation & financial problems through PSW
Intermediate

Biological

  • For Alzheimer's: AchE-I alone sufficient for mild-moderate; AchE-I + memantine if severe
  • Donepezil 5mg nocte/mane → ↑10mg after 4 weeks
  • or Rivastigmine 1.5mg bd → ↑ to 3-6mg bd
  • If severe: add Memantine 5mg mane, ↑5mg weekly to 20mg
  • BPSD (severe only): Risperidone 0.5mg n or Quetiapine 12.5mg n, max 3 months
  • Co-morbid depression: Sertraline (caution — hyponatraemia)
  • Sleep: Melatonin 3mg 1-2hrs before bed — do NOT give BDZ
  • Screen for metabolic disease: FBS, lipid profile, BP → medical referral if abnormal
  • Hearing/vision impairment → appropriate referral

Psychological

  • Cognitive stimulation therapy with OT involvement — group therapy, singing

  • Drug SE assessment
  • Metabolic Ix

  • AchEIs: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, LOW/LOA, muscle weakness

SCZ

Schizophrenia

1st rank symptoms, antipsychotics, clozapine & EPSE

History Pearls

  • Intro includes nearest hospital & distance
  • PC ex: hearing voices of unseen people, aggressive behaviour & suspiciousness
  • 1st rank symptoms — Thought (insertion/withdrawal/broadcast), Delusion of control, Delusional perception (different meaning to a real perception, not always directed at self unlike delusion of reference)
  • Bizarre delusions — completely impossible things e.g. superhuman powers, controlling the weather
  • 3rd person AH, or 2nd person running commentary; hallucinatory voices from body parts
  • Persistent hallucinations (>2), incoherent/irrelevant speech, neologisms, catatonic behaviour, negative symptoms — duration >1 month
  • Negative symptoms: anhedonia, social withdrawal. Cognitive symptoms: memory impairment, impaired A&C
  • Impact of life & level of functioning
  • Past psych: reason for this relapse → poor compliance, ongoing stressors, high EE, substance use

Diagnostic Criteria

  • DD — Schizoaffective (mood symptoms), PDD (single clear delusion >3 months), BPAD (↑ energy)
  • Substance-induced psychosis, Organic (hypo/hyperthyroid, headache from space-occupying lesion)

MSE & Risk

  • A: poor self care, idiosyncratic features. B: self mumbling, hand gestures, disinhibition, overfamiliarity
  • Speech: rate/tone/amount/volume, neologism, flight of ideas
  • Mood/Affect: range, congruence, reactivity, lability
  • Thought: delusions, obsessional, suicidal/homicidal. Perception: hallucinations
  • Risk — self: neglect, dehydration/nutrition, suicide, reputation, relapse. Others: homicide/self harm, education for children. From others: financial/physical exploitation

Management

Acute

Biological

  • 1. Decide setting
  • 2. If aggressive → Mx
  • 3. Ensure safe environment
  • 4. Pharmacological mx — atypical & typical similar efficacy
  • Risperidone 2mg n (Max 16mg)
  • Olanzapine 5-10mg n start (Max 20mg)
  • Quetiapine 400-600mg n (Max 750mg)
  • Haloperidol 2mg n (Max 20mg)
  • TFP 10mg (Max 20mg)
  • Chlorpromazine 10mg n (Max 20mg)
  • If non-sedative agent given, can add short course O. Clonazepam 0.5mg n
  • If risk ↑↑, ECT is also an option

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation to family members
  • Ix before starting SGA: FBS, Lipid profile at start & 4-6 monthly (Olanzapine at 1 month then 4-6 monthly)
  • ECG, QTc prolongation

Social

  • Collateral hx
  • Assess family support
Intermediate

Biological

  • 1. Titrate the dose
  • Do MSE & risk assessments during ward rounds
  • 3. Assess SEs
  • 4. If co-morbid, monitor & refer if needed
  • If no response to adequate trial (4-6 weeks) → switch to another drug class
  • 5. Mx of substance use → motivational interviewing

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation once stable
  • Assess cause of poor compliance
  • Healthy lifestyle, sleep; ↓ high EE (critical comments, hostility, over-involvement); ↓ psychological stressors
  • Identify early features of relapse
  • Plan discharge with 2-week review & clinic followup

Social

  • Functional assessment & OT
  • Plan & rehabilitation
Long term

Biological

  • Continue Rx: 1st episode 1-2yrs after remission; 2nd episode 5 years; multiple episodes → may be lifelong
  • If no response after adequate trials of drugs → Resistant schizophrenia → start Clozapine 6.25mg evening, titrate up
  • If poor compliance: simplify regimen, consider LAIs
  • Monitor for medication SEs

Psychological

  • Family therapy
  • Coping skills improvement
  • Stress management

Social

  • Support from CPN if poor compliance
  • Rehabilitation if indicated
  • Financial support through PSW
  • Support for finding accommodation
  • Track functional level between episodes

Aggressive Patient — Escalation Ladder

  • 1. Verbal de-escalation
  • 2. Oral: Risperidone 2-4mg / Olanzapine 5-10mg / Haloperidol 5-10mg + Promethazine 25mg / Chlorpromazine 50-100mg / Oral BDZ (Lorazepam 2mg, Clonazepam 2mg, Midazolam 10mg)
  • 3. IM: Olanzapine 5-10mg IM (watch hypotension) / Haloperidol 5-10mg + Promethazine 25mg IM (watch EPSE, QT, NMS) — monitor BP/PR/RR ¼-hrly then ½-hrly then hrly until awake
  • 4. Failed → physical restraining

Clozapine Essentials

  • Indication: resistant schizophrenia (1/3 respond completely)
  • Before starting: BMI, FBC, FBS, lipid profile, ECG, 2D echo
  • Start 12.5mg n/bd → titrate daily; max dose 900mg/day (targets differ by sex/smoking status)
  • Monitor: BP (hypotension risk), PR tds, T° (fever/myocarditis/agranulocytosis)
  • Common SEs: sedation, orthostatic hypotension, hypersalivation (Benzhexol 2mg mane), constipation (can → paralytic ileus — fluids, fibre, Bisacodyl then lactulose), weight gain, DM/DL, tachycardia & fever settle by 4 weeks
  • Serious rare SEs: agranulocytosis, myocarditis/cardiomyopathy, fulminant hepatic failure, pancreatitis, pericarditis
  • Monitoring: FBC weekly ×18 weeks then monthly (watch fever/sore throat/malaise); FBS & lipids at 1 month then 4-6 monthly
  • Missed dose: 1 day → continue; ≥2 days (48hrs) → restart titration from beginning

EPSE Management

  • Acute dystonia (hours): IV/IM Benztropine 2mg or Benzhexol
  • Akathisia (hrs-wks): ↓ dose, change drug, low-dose propranolol, Clonazepam 0.5mg (anticholinergics unhelpful)
  • Parkinsonism (days-wk): ↓ dose, switch to lower-propensity drug, Benzhexol 2mg
  • Tardive dyskinesia (months-yrs): stop anticholinergic, ↓ AP dose, switch drug

NMS — Fatal Emergency

  • Fever, sweating, muscle rigidity, confusion, fluctuating consciousness, labile/↑BP, tachycardia
  • Ix: CK >1000, leukocytosis, altered LFTs — confirm with ↑CPK
  • Mx: withdraw antipsychotic, monitor T°/PR/BP, BDZ, hydration, bromocriptine + dantrolene, ventilation if needed, ECT may help
  • Restart AP after ~5 days with a low dopamine-affinity agent, avoid depot preparations

Poor Prognostic Factors & ECT

  • Single & male, co-morbid substance use, poor compliance, family hx, insidious onset, long duration untreated
  • If not controlled with clozapine: combination of antipsychotics, or ECT
  • ECT prep: written consent, RFT/SE/ECG, cognitive assessment, fasting (solid 6h, clear liquid 2h), remove dentures/jewellery
  • ECT CI: any illness ↑ anaesthetic risk (recent MI, HF, cerebral/aortic aneurysm, DVT)
  • ECT complications: headache, nausea, vertigo, muscle pain, retro/anterograde amnesia, brief disorientation, cardiac arrhythmias
DEP

Depression & Post Partum

ICD-10 criteria, drug choice & postpartum spectrum

History Pearls

  • PC may follow a suicidal attempt / overdose in background of persistent low mood & ↓energy
  • Elicit: persistent low mood (diurnal variation), ↓energy/activity, lack of interest/enjoyment, ↓concentration, ↓self esteem, guilt/unworthiness, pessimism, ideas of self harm, sleep disturbance, ↓appetite
  • Somatic symptoms: constipation, fatigue, bodyache
  • Psychotic symptoms — delusions & hallucinations; past depressive/manic episodes
  • Exclude anxiety disorders, OCD, hypothyroidism, coexistent substance use
  • Impact: day to day life, relationships, occupation, education, children
  • Premorbid — borderline traits: fear of abandonment, unstable but intense relationships, impulsivity (spending/sex/substance/driving), recurrent self harm, rapid mood changes, chronic emptiness

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Major: depressed mood, ↓energy/activity, loss of interest/enjoyment
  • Minor: ↓concentration, ↓self esteem, guilt/unworthiness, pessimism, self harm ideas, disturbed sleep, ↓appetite
  • 2A+2B → Mild, 2A+3B → Moderate, 3A+4B → Severe
  • Present most of the day, most days, for ≥2 weeks

MSE & Risk

  • A&B: self care, self harm injuries, psychomotor retardation, eye contact & rapport
  • Speech: poverty of speech. Mood/Affect. Thought: suicidal, homicidal, obsessional, delusions
  • Cognitive distortions: overgeneralization, arbitrary inference, selective abstraction, personalization
  • Risk: suicidal/homicidal/DSH (detailed assessment), negligence/self care, violence/domestic violence, risk to children

Management

Acute

Biological

  • Decide setting per risk/severity/support system
  • Ix's not needed to start
  • If high risk: bed near nursing station, observe for self harm/food intake/hydration
  • Start O. Sertraline 50mg mane
  • If sleep difficulty → short course Clonazepam 0.5mg
  • Emotional ventilation with trained counsellor
  • If psychotic symptoms → add antipsychotic

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation: not pt's fault, psychiatric illness, Rx options, importance of compliance
  • Medications take ~2-4 weeks to work; importance of family support & adherence
  • Psychotherapies with OT: Interpersonal therapy, CBT (cognitive restructuring), Supportive psychotherapy & problem solving, Behavioural activation

Social

  • Through PSW assess home environment; ensure safety for pt & children
  • Discuss what kind of help pt needs — occupational training, relationships, education
Intermediate

Biological

  • Daily MSE & risk assessment
  • Consider titrating dose after 2 weeks (75mg, max 200)
  • Discharge once risk minimal & symptoms improved
  • Arrange clinic followup, assess improvement & SEs
  • Continue drugs ~6 months after remission
  • Motivational interviewing for substance use

Psychological

  • DSH mx if having — combine therapies with discussion of support system & crisis plan
  • Continue this during intermediate mx as well

Social

—

Drug Choice by Scenario

  • Mild-moderate: Sertraline 50mg mane (max 200mg)
  • Severe: Venlafaxine 75mg or Sertraline 50mg (venlafaxine slightly more effective)
  • Pregnancy/lactation: Sertraline 1st line
  • Elderly: Mirtazapine best (Bupropion if hyponatraemia risk)
  • Post-MI: Sertraline best
  • Other options: Fluoxetine 20mg (max 60mg), Venlafaxine 75mg (max 375mg div), TCAs, Mirtazapine 30mg (max 45mg)
  • 1st drug 2wks no response → ↑dose 2wks → no response → switch drug

Antidepressant Side Effects

  • SSRI: nausea, diarrhoea, dyspepsia, dry mouth, headache, insomnia, dizziness, sweating, erectile dysfunction, delayed orgasm, hyponatraemia (SIADH) — often settle in 1-2 months. CI: liver/renal failure
  • SNRI: dose-related ↑BP/HR (monitor, caution if hypertensive), high insomnia/anxiety/agitation, more cardiotoxic than SSRI
  • TCA: dry mouth, constipation, tachycardia, urine retention, dry skin, postural hypotension. Overdose → tachyarrhythmias & intracardiac blocks
  • Mirtazapine (NaSSA): ↑appetite, weight gain, drowsiness, dry mouth

Postpartum Spectrum

  • Blues: 50-70% get, irritability/labile mood/crying spells, day 3-4, resolves spontaneously
  • PPD: onset usually within 2 weeks, prominent tiredness/irritability/anxiety/phobic symptoms, obsessional fears of harming baby; most recover 2-6 months but 30% persist; screen with Edinburgh Postnatal Depression scale at 4-6 weeks; high risk (40%) of relapse
  • Postpartum psychosis: onset 2-3 days postpartum (1-2 per 1000 births), more common in primigravida, sudden onset & rapid deterioration, mood symptoms predominant (mania/mixed/depressive), needs inpatient care, mother-baby contact supervised
  • Psychosis mx: antipsychotics (Olanzapine 5mg n / Risperidone / Quetiapine 25-50mg), Lithium if clear bipolar component (not if breastfeeding), ECT if very high risk

Serotonin Syndrome & Resistant Depression

  • Mild: insomnia, anxiety, nausea, diarrhoea, hypertension, tachycardia, hyperreflexia
  • Moderate: agitation, myoclonus, tremor, mydriasis, flushing, diaphoresis, low-grade fever
  • Severe: hyperthermia, confusion — caution during cross-tapering
  • Resistant depression: ensure compliance & adequate dose; ↑dose; combine 2 antidepressants with 2 mechanisms (SSRI+Mirtazapine, SNRI+Mirtazapine, SSRI+Bupropion); augment with antipsychotic; combine with ECT

Pregnancy, Breastfeeding & Stress Disorders

  • Sertraline preferred in pregnancy & lactation; Fluoxetine/Venlafaxine — slightly higher neonatal withdrawal risk; TCAs safe in pregnancy
  • Neonatal poor adaptation syndrome: 20-30% of SSRI/SNRI-exposed infants — irritability, tremor, poor feeding, tachypnoea, rarely seizures, starts within hours
  • Acute stress reaction: minutes onset, resolves 2-3 days. Adjustment disorder: onset <1 month, usually <6 months
  • PTSD: triad — nightmares, hypervigilance, flashbacks, avoidance. Mx: trauma-focused CBT, cognitive restructuring; SSRI/SNRI ± antipsychotic augmentation
SUD

Heroin / Substance Use

Dependence, withdrawal & relapse prevention

History Pearls

  • PC: referred from another ward or self admission
  • Explain substance use: onset & circumstances, usage pattern (frequency/amount), tolerance, route, spend
  • Dependence: strong desire, inability to control, tolerance, withdrawal symptoms, neglect of pleasurable activities, continued use despite harm
  • Withdrawal: nausea/vomiting, anxiety, insomnia, hot/cold flushes, perspiration, muscle cramps, watery discharge from eyes/nose, diarrhoea
  • Impact: intoxication/respiratory difficulty, psychotic symptoms, occupation, family life, STDs, law enforcement
  • Co-morbid depression/anxiety; previous abstinence attempts & why relapsed; motivational stage; other substance use
  • Pre-morbid personality: novelty-seeking, reward dependency, childhood conduct disorder, impulsivity

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Dependence criteria: strong desire, inability to control use, tolerance, neglect of pleasurable activities, continued use despite known harm

MSE & Risk

  • Insight — motivational stage (pre-contemplation / contemplation / preparation / action / maintenance / relapse)
  • Risk — self: suicidal, self harm, self neglect, physical health (STDs), reputation, financial, job loss, accidents
  • Risk — others: violence/homicidal, domestic violence, to children. From others: physical & sexual exploitation

Management

Acute

Biological

  • Heroin withdrawal is not lethal — nothing to achieve by keeping in ward
  • Setting depends on: risk level, motivational stage, medical/psychiatric co-morbidity, family support
  • Most managed as outpatient
  • Nausea/vomiting: Domperidone 10mg tds/PRN or Metoclopramide 10mg tds/PRN
  • Muscle pain: PCM 1g QDS/PRN, Diclofenac 50mg tds, PCM+codeine 1g QDS
  • Diarrhoea: Loperamide 4mg then 2mg per loose stool (max 16mg/day)
  • Agitation/poor sleep: Promethazine 25mg tds (max 75mg/day), Chlorpromazine 50mg qds (max 300mg/day), or Olanzapine 5-10mg
  • GORD: Omeprazole 20mg bd
  • Nasal congestion: Chlorphenamine 4mg tds (max 24mg/day), Cetirizine 5mg tds
  • Anxiety: Diazepam 5-10mg QDS/PRN (max 40mg/day) for 7 days
  • Address nutritional problems

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation — withdrawal not lethal, disappears after 5-7 days
  • Motivational interviewing — non-judgemental, empathetic; help identify pleasurable vs harmful aspects; set goals; move to next stage of motivational cycle
  • Assess caregiver psychological aspect

Social

  • Through PSW address domestic violence or legal issues
  • Help find a job
  • Arrange rehabilitation if significant functional impairment or legal issues
  • Help prevent stigma & connect with family/society
Long term

Biological

  • Relapse prevention: O. Naltrexone start 25mg, monitor 4hrs, then ↑ to 50mg/day
  • Start 1-2 weeks after stopping (avoid precipitating withdrawal)
  • Warn: taking heroin while on naltrexone → precipitates intoxication
  • Methadone & Buprenorphine not used in Sri Lanka
  • Rx co-morbid depression/anxiety; STD screening
  • Naltrexone SEs: nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, ↓appetite, diarrhoea, headache, dizziness, fatigue, low mood. CI: hepatic dysfunction (monitor LFT) — course ~6 months

Psychological

  • Continue motivational interviewing at follow up
  • CBT & OT to prevent relapse: identify triggers, urge surfing, practice saying 'no', distracting activities
  • Help find meaning/goal in life beyond substance use
  • If relapse occurs: appreciate they returned, understand without blaming, frame as part of recovery, involve PSW

Social

  • Withdrawal starts 4-6 hrs after last dose, lasts 5-7 days

Heroin Intoxication

  • Breathing difficulty, RR <12, unconsciousness, pinpoint pupils, cyanosis, cold clammy skin

Opioid Substitution (not used in Sri Lanka)

  • Methadone — full agonist, 60-100mg/day
  • Buprenorphine — partial agonist, 12-24mg/day
  • Not given due to high maintenance cost

Other Substances

  • Tobacco: nicotine receptors adapt quickly (early withdrawal); nicotine 150mg/day to ↓craving
  • Cannabis (ganja/marijuana/hashish): mild withdrawal (irritability, nausea, insomnia, anorexia); psychosis similar to schizophrenia; increases relapse severity of psychiatric illness
  • Methamphetamine: withdrawal = low mood, ↓energy, depression, anxiety, tremulousness, lethargy, fatigue, nightmares; Mx = MI & CBT, BDZ if severe
OCD

OCD & Anxiety Disorders

ICD criteria, cognitive distortions & management

History Pearls

  • OCD PC ex: gradual onset progressive recurrent distressing thoughts/doubts/images/ruminations followed by compulsion for 3 months
  • OCD: describes thoughts as senseless, unpleasant, own thought, fail to resist; compulsive act; ↑anxiety/panic if not performed
  • Social anxiety: marked fear/discomfort in social situations, feels observed/judged, avoidance, anticipatory anxiety (begins hrs-days before event)
  • Agoraphobia: fear situations where escape difficult/help unavailable
  • Specific phobia: excessive fear restricted to one object/situation (animal, height, blood)
  • GAD: excessive pervasive worry on most days ≥6 months about multiple issues (health/work/finance), not specific to a situation; restlessness, dry mouth, sleep disturbance, muscle tension, poor concentration
  • Panic disorder: recurrent unexpected episodes of racing heart, chest tightness, choking, fear of dying — no specific trigger

Diagnostic Criteria

  • OCD: symptoms recognized as own thoughts/impulses, cannot resist, not pleasurable, unpleasantly repetitive, most of the time over past 2 weeks
  • Social anxiety: restricted to particular social situations, psych/behavioural/autonomic symptoms, situations avoided when possible
  • Agoraphobia: psych & autonomic anxiety symptoms, restricted to ≥2 of crowds/public places/travelling alone, avoidance
  • Specific phobia: psych & autonomic symptoms restricted to the phobic object/situation, avoidance
  • Panic disorder: several autonomic anxiety attacks within past month, no objective danger, not confined to specific situations, no anxiety between attacks
  • GAD: primary anxiety symptoms for several weeks — apprehension, motor tension, autonomic overactivity

MSE & Risk

  • Physical: tremor, sweating, dry mouth, GIT symptoms. Psychological: excessive worry, irritability, fearful anticipation, apprehension, restlessness
  • OCD cognitive distortions: thought-action fusion, inflated responsibility, perfectionism, intolerance of uncertainty
  • Anxiety cognitive distortions: catastrophization, overestimation of threat, selective abstraction, all-or-none thinking, intolerance of uncertainty, emotional reasoning, mind reading
  • Risk: self — suicide/self harm, deterioration of academic/occupational function; risk to others is low

Management

OCD — Acute

Biological

  • Mx setting: outpatient if no significant risk; inpatient if risk↑
  • 1st line SSRI: Sertraline 50mg m (educate — takes 10-12 weeks to show improvement, max 200mg)
  • 2nd line: Fluoxetine 20mg m (max 60-80mg)
  • 3rd line: Clomipramine (TCA) 25mg mane (max 250mg)

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation: curable, illness nature, importance of family support
  • Behavioural therapy — Exposure Response Prevention: list obsessional thoughts & anxiety level, expose to mild-anxiety situation without compulsion, let anxiety rise then fall (20-40 min), repeat as homework, record anxiety level, avoid reassurance
  • Cognitive therapy — restructure identified distortions with evidence

OCD — Intermediate/Long term

Biological

  • Continue pharmacological mx up to 1 yr after remission
  • Mx co-morbid depression & substance use

Psychological

  • Continue CBT; regular follow up (MSE, drug SE, compliance)
  • Support for education/functional impairment & OT
  • Advice on healthy lifestyle — sleep, diet, exercise
  • Educate on early identification of relapse (↑symptoms)

Social Anxiety

Biological

  • If therapy alone insufficient: SSRI — Sertraline start 25mg mane
  • Short course BDZ initially (anxiety ↑ before it ↓): Clonazepam 0.5mg n or Alprazolam 0.25mg n
  • Continue SSRI 6-12 months after remission

Psychological

  • Set concrete goals with pt (e.g. continue studies without pre-occupation)
  • Psychoeducation: not pt's fault, treatable illness
  • Graded exposure with OT: build hierarchy of anxiety-provoking situations, expose from mild → most avoided, repeat until anxiety naturally reduces, record anxiety (1-10 scale), avoid reassurance
  • Cognitive restructuring for overestimation of threat / underestimation of coping / selective abstraction
  • Same structure used for specific phobia & agoraphobia (e.g. cockroach: imagine → image → dead one → non-flying → flying)

OH

Alcohol Use Disorder

Units, withdrawal & detoxification management

History Pearls

  • Unit calculation: Gal Arrack = 32u/L (1 bottle=750ml); illicit (25-40%) ≈40u/L; beer 6u/L; toddy 3u/L; expensive spirits ~40u/L
  • Drinking pattern: type, amount, duration, frequency, circumstances, last drink, describe a typical day
  • Dependence criteria: strong desire, inability to control, physiological withdrawal, tolerance, neglect of activities, persistent use despite harm
  • Withdrawal — uncomplicated: tremors, sweating, N/V, anxiety, insomnia. Complicated: seizures, DT (confusion, vivid hallucinations), WE (confusion, ataxia)
  • Psychotic symptoms: VH/AH, delusions (esp. infidelity); co-morbid depression, suicidal ideas
  • Impact: financial, physical (injuries/accidents), medical (CVD/CLD/pancreatitis), family (domestic violence, sexual dysfunction), social (forensic, friends)
  • Previous abstinence attempts, withdrawal symptoms experienced, where detox done; motivational level (pre-contemplation → relapse)
  • Pre-morbid: introvert/extrovert, coping skills, risk-taking/novelty-seeking, impulsivity

MSE & Risk

  • A: build, age, self care, withdrawal features, anxiety. Speech, mood, thought, perception, cognition, insight
  • Risk — self: suicidal/self harm, physical health, reputation, finances. Others: violence, children, RTA
  • From others: physical & sexual exploitation
  • Exam: BMI, parotid swelling, hepatosplenomegaly, cerebellar signs (gait, nystagmus, finger-nose test)

Management

Acute

Biological

  • 1. Assess severity of withdrawal symptoms
  • 2. Ix: FBC (nutrition), AST/ALT/GGT/INR (liver injury), FBS & lipids if not done, LFTs
  • 3. Detoxification (Maudsley regimen usually if CIWA >15)
  • Chlordiazepoxide fixed-dose regimen with gradual taper: dose based on daily unit intake, QDS oral, max 40mg QDS +10mg PRN up to 200mg/day
  • Severe dependence: taper over 7-10 days; mild-moderate: 5-7 days
  • 4. Hydration
  • 5. Thiamine: 300mg/day oral if mild/mod (~1 month); 300mg/day IV if severe (~5 days, then convert to oral)
  • 6. Monitor BP/PR/RR/SpO2 daily
  • 7. Observe for severe withdrawal: confusion, seizures, ataxia, vertigo
  • 8. Ensure adequate nutrition

  • Risk to children

Intermediate

Biological

  • Medical referrals if needed
  • Plan for discharge
  • Support for abstinence: anti-craving, aversion therapy
  • Naltrexone — 1st line but not available in our setup
  • Acamprosate — 2nd line, not available in our setup
  • Disulfiram — 3rd line: start 800mg/day → ↓ to 100-800mg/day maintenance, 3-6 months (can be longer)

Psychological

  • Motivational interviewing: discuss pleasurable vs harmful aspects, encourage change, move to next stage of motivational cycle
  • CBT: identify triggers/circumstances/thoughts; coping with craving (don't fight it, distraction); plan for high-risk situations (different route home if bar on the way); train refusal skills; emergency/crisis plan — leave place, discard remaining alcohol, contact someone; educate that despite relapse, continue Rx
  • Address co-morbid depression/delusional disorder; improve adaptive coping strategies

Social

  • Occupational help

Withdrawal Management (Chlordiazepoxide)

  • SE: drowsiness, dizziness, respiratory depression. CI: advanced liver failure (can decompensate)
  • Alternatives if CDZ unsuitable: Lorazepam 1mg 2-4hrly, Oxazepam 60mg 2-4hrs, Diazepam 10mg 4hrly
  • If BDZ can't be given: Carbamazepine can be used

Thiamine Prophylaxis

  • Mild-moderate withdrawal: oral 300mg daily. Severe: IV/IM 300mg daily ~5 days
  • Suspected WE/DT: IV/IM 500mg/day for 1-5 days, then oral 1-3 months or until good nutrition
  • IV carries risk of anaphylaxis

Relapse Prevention Drugs

  • Disulfiram: inhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase → accumulation of acetaldehyde → disulfiram reaction if alcohol taken
  • Reaction: mild — flushing, sweating, nausea, dyspnoea, tachycardia, hypotension; severe — AF/MI/arrhythmia/resp depression
  • CI: recent intake <24h, CVD, HTN, stroke, pregnancy, BF, severe liver disease, peripheral neuropathy — needs consent
  • Naltrexone: opioid blockade prevents ↑dopaminergic reward from alcohol; 25mg then 50mg/day. CI: acute liver failure
  • Acamprosate: NMDA antagonist + ↑GABA; 1998mg/day (>60kg) or 1332mg/day (<60kg). CI: severe renal/liver failure
  • Topiramate: 25mg → 50mg → 75mg weekly increments (effective dose)

DT & Wernicke's — Emergencies

  • Timeline: mild sx (agitation/anxiety/irritability) 12hr-14days; seizures 24-48hrs; DT 72-96hrs (lasts 3-4 days)
  • Alcohol withdrawal seizure: exclude hypoglycaemia/electrolyte imbalance/infection; manage/increase CDZ
  • DT (medical emergency): confusion, vivid hallucinations, marked tremors, autonomic hyperactivity, agitation, insomnia
  • DT Mx: HDU, monitor vitals & fluid balance, Diazepam 10-20mg PO/IV repeat 1-2hrly until calm (or Lorazepam), IV/IM Thiamine 500mg, electrolyte correction
  • Wernicke's: ataxia, confusion, ophthalmoplegia/nystagmus, hypothermia, hypotension, memory disturbance
  • WE Mx: high-dose IV thiamine 400-600mg tds ×2 days then 200-300mg daily ×5 days
BPAD

Bipolar Affective Disorder

ICD-10 mania/hypomania, MSE, lithium & antipsychotics

History Pearls

  • PC: ↓sleep, ↑aggression, overtalkativeness, overexpensiveness
  • Explore: which part of sleep is difficult, level of function before episode, GID/PD, goal-directed behaviour, hallucinations, ↑libido
  • Past depressive & manic episodes; exclude schizophrenia 1st rank symptoms & substance-induced psychosis
  • Organic causes: hypo/hyperthyroid, corticosteroids, fever, headache, head injuries
  • Personal hx: was she a person who sought centre of attention? Why did she leave jobs? Marriage problems?
  • Past psych hx: when diagnosed, symptoms, Rx (oral/IM/ECT), SEs, compliance & reasons for poor compliance, number of relapses/admissions, other psychiatric illness, suicide attempts, last admission

Diagnostic Criteria

  • Mania: elevated mood (sometimes irritable/suspicious), ↑energy, disturbed work & social activities, for at least 1 week
  • Associated: ↑activity, ↓sleep, pressure of speech, excessive optimism, grandiosity
  • When telling dx: 'BPAD, current episode manic, with/without psychotic symptoms'
  • Hypomania: lesser degree — persistent mild mood elevation, ↑energy/activity, talkativeness, ↑sociability, overfamiliarity, ↑sexual energy, poor sleep, but NO psychotic symptoms; daily activities not disrupted; lasts several days

MSE & Risk

  • A&B: look/age/BMI/dress/hygiene/make-up/accessories, idiosyncratic features; overfamiliarity, disinhibition, hand gestures, abnormal movements
  • Speech: spontaneous/coherent/relevant, great pressure of speech, loosening of associations
  • Mood: elated/irritable — why? Affect: reactive, range, congruent, labile
  • Thought: delusions (conviction, rational arguments, cannot be explained culturally), suicidal/homicidal, obsessional
  • Risk to self: overspending, physical exhaustion, self negligence, reputation, prone to injuries, suicide/relapse
  • Risk to/from others: violence; financial/sexual/physical exploitation from others

Management

Acute

Biological

  • 1. Decide setting
  • 2. If aggressive → Mx
  • 3. Ensure safe environment
  • 4. Ix: FBC
  • 5. Pharmacological mx
  • Risperidone 2mg nocte (Max 16mg)
  • Olanzapine 5-10mg nocte (Max 20mg)
  • Haloperidol 3mg tds (Max 30mg)
  • Quetiapine 50mg bd then ↑ max 800mg
  • Clonazepam 0.5mg n or Alprazolam 0.25mg bd (short course, not available in govt sector) if disturbed sleep
  • Mood stabilizer — Na Valproate 200mg bd (Max 2g), or Li 400-600mg nocte ↑ weekly (target 0.8-1.0) Max 1g
  • 1 tab Li₂CO₃ = 250mg

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation to family — may not help patient in acute setting; maintain calm ward environment
  • Ix before starting one of the 4 antipsychotics: FBS, Lipid profile at start & 4-6 monthly (Olanzapine 1 month then 4-6 monthly)
  • Lithium: TFT, RFT (eGFR, S.Ca) at start & every 6 months; loading dose 20mg/kg can be given before
  • Na valproate: FBC, LFT initially, RFT — 3-monthly FBC/LFT in 1st year then annually

Social

  • Collateral hx
  • Assess family support & background
Intermediate

Biological

  • Titrate doses
  • Do MSE & risk assessments during ward rounds
  • Assess SEs
  • If co-morbidities, monitor (DM — daily CBS, HTN — BP); medical referral if not controlled
  • Clozapine if resistant

Psychological

  • Psychoeducation to patient once stable

Social

  • Functional assessment with occupational therapist
Long term

Biological

  • Consider discharge once stable
  • Continue drugs: antipsychotics → 6 months after remission
  • Mood stabilizer: 1st episode 2yrs after remission, 2nd episode 5yrs, multiple episodes → lifelong
  • Review in 2 weeks; clinic followup monitoring symptoms & drug SE
  • Long term injectables if poor compliance

Psychological

  • Educate family, ↓high EE
  • Importance of healthy lifestyle — good sleep, stress mx
  • Identify early features of relapse: ↓sleep, overtalkativeness, overspending — bring in early

Social

  • Get support from CPN if poor compliance
  • Rehabilitation if needed
  • Financial support through PSW (e.g. allowance, finding a job)

Lithium Essentials

  • 1 tab = 250mg — give nearest dose. Good for both mania & depression; needs good adherence
  • Before starting: TFTs, RFTs, BMI. Start 400-600mg n (Max 2g), increase weekly
  • Check level 12hrs post-dose & 7 days after each change (no fasting needed)
  • Expected levels — acute: 0.8-1.0 mmol/L; prophylaxis: 0.6-0.75 mmol/L
  • SEs: fine tremor, polyuria, weight gain, arrhythmias; long term — renal impairment, hypothyroidism, hyperparathyroidism
  • Toxicity: >1.5 nausea/diarrhoea/drowsiness/ataxia/coarse tremor; >2mmol/L seizures; >3mmol/L → haemodialysis
  • Pregnancy: not absolutely contraindicated; can cause Ebstein's anomaly; mild stable BPAD → taper during pregnancy; moderate risk → taper during T1 with informed consent; high risk → continue
  • Can cause rebound mania if stopped abruptly; missing 2 days needs re-titration

Lithium Toxicity Grades

  • Mild: lethargy, N/V, diarrhoea, tremors, muscle weakness
  • Moderate: confusion, dysarthria, nystagmus, hyperreflexia, ataxia, fasciculation
  • Severe: seizures, coma, stupor, cardiopulmonary collapse, myoclonus, hypertonicity, rigidity, resistant hypotension
  • Mx: stop lithium, hydrate with 0.9% NaCl (prevent hyponatraemia & further toxicity), check level

Antipsychotic Side Effects

  • Risperidone: sedation, weight gain, dizziness, dry mouth, constipation, sexual dysfunction, hyperprolactinaemia, EPSE, metabolic SEs
  • Olanzapine: weight gain, sedation, DM/DL, postural dizziness, dry mouth/constipation/tachycardia, less EPSE risk but ↑prolactin, rare hepatitis/pancreatitis
  • Quetiapine: sedation, dizziness, dry mouth, weight gain, DM/DL, constipation, ↑liver enzymes, tachycardia, headache, ↓EPSE risk
  • Hyperprolactinaemia: amenorrhoea, oligomenorrhoea, galactorrhoea, infertility, ↓libido, vaginal dryness, erectile dysfunction, gynaecomastia

Depot Antipsychotics

  • Fluphenazine decanoate: 1 vial 1ml=25mg. 1st dose 12.5mg IM (gluteal/deltoid) → after 4-7 days another 12.5mg → then monthly or 2-weekly 25mg, ↑ per response. Max 100mg 2-weekly (dose ↑ considered every 4-6 weeks)
  • Flupentixol decanoate: 20mg stat → repeat 20mg in 5 days → 40mg after 1 month → ↑4-weekly per response. Max 400mg weekly

Bipolar Depression & Schizoaffective Disorder

  • If depressive episode develops while on Li/valproate: ↑dose to max therapeutic range first (Li until 0.8-1 mmol/L; valproate up to 2g/day)
  • If unsuccessful: 1st line Fluoxetine 20-40mg + Olanzapine 5-10mg (NICE); 2nd Quetiapine 300-600mg daily; 3rd Olanzapine alone; 4th Lamotrigine 200mg/day
  • Stop add-on after 3-6 months remission; continue Li/valproate as mood stabilizer
  • Schizoaffective manic type: Olanzapine 10mg/day / Quetiapine 100mg/day / Risperidone; add mood stabilizer if antipsychotic alone insufficient (Li 400mg/day or Na valproate 200mg bd)
  • Schizoaffective depressive type: Olanzapine+Fluoxetine combination, Quetiapine monotherapy, or Lamotrigine
