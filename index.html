These pages are a machine-assisted transcription (OCR) of handwritten case sheets, corrected for legibility while preserving the original wording as closely as possible. Passages highlighted in red within each case were difficult to read in the original scan (numbers, doses, place names) — please verify these against the original notes. The teal "Oxford Guidance" boxes throughout summarise the corresponding section of the Shorter Oxford Textbook of Psychiatry (Cowen, Harrison & Burns) for quick cross-reference, and are not part of the original case notes.

About This Casebook

How each case is structured

Every case page follows the same six tabs, so you can jump straight to what you need before a viva:

  • History — introduction, presenting complaint, HOPC, family/personal/past psychiatric history, premorbid personality, and risk assessment.
  • MSE — the full mental state examination.
  • Physical Exam & DDx — examination findings and differential diagnosis.
  • Summary & Problem List — a concise case summary and biological/psychological/social problem list.
  • Management — immediate, intermediate, and long-term management.
  • Drug Side Effects — side-effect profiles for the medications used in that case.

Teal "Oxford Guidance" boxes are placed at the key diagnostic and management decision points in each case, summarising the relevant section of the Shorter Oxford Textbook of Psychiatry so the underlying textbook reasoning sits right next to the clinical example.
