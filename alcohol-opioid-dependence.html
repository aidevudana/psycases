Habit Breaking Plan

For a person in contemplation & action stages.

  1. Write down benefits of cutting down or stopping alcohol
  2. Identify at least 4 high risk situations which might influence the person to drink
  3. Identify at least 2 strategies to avoid or resist each high risk situation

Motivational Interviewing — 3 steps: ORAS

  1. O — Engage
  2. E — Evoke (evoke change talk — don't encourage sustained talk)
  3. P — Plan (get the plan from the patient; identify situations & solutions)

OARS

  • O — Open ended questions
  • R — Reflection
  • A — Affirmation & Appreciate things in the patient
  • S — Summarise
  • Do not start the interview with negative things
  • Let the person take a good look at the mirror → change talk & sustained talk
  • Try to direct the patient to change talk
  • Explain the advantages & disadvantages & let the patient decide
  • Ask the patient what can he do? what is best for him?
  • Train refusal skills & assertiveness training — refuse drinks/cigars, saying no to something undesirable
  • Preparation for urge surfing — craving comes in waves, lasts 15–20 minutes. Instead of alcohol, can drink fruit juice etc.

Brief Intervention (CBT)

  • Functional analysis of substance use — triggers / cues / feelings / behaviour
  • Coping with craving & boredom — urge surfing
  • Identify & deal with high risk situations — habit breaking plan
  • Managing thoughts about substance use
  • Training in substance refusal skills
  • Planning for emergencies

Relapse Prevention

  • Non-pharmacological — CBT / Motivational enhancement therapy / Brief intervention / MI
  • Pharmacological
    • Acamprosate (↓ craving)
    • Disulfiram
    • Naltrexone
    • Topiramate
More effective when combined with psychological therapy — motivational enhancement & MI.

04 · Detoxification of Dependence / Withdrawal Symptoms (CCD2P)

  1. Assess the severity — CIWA scale / SAW scale
  2. Mild → No / Low chlordiazepoxide
  3. Moderate → 10–20 mg 6-hourly, O. Taper in 5–7 days
  4. Severe → In-patient Rx · 30 mg 6-hourly, O. Taper in 7–10 days
  5. Symptomatic → 20–30 mg stat. Monitor hourly. Taper 20% daily. Stop at day 6.

Chlordiazepoxide (CDPX) Regime

Fixed dose regime — usually practised in Sri Lanka.

  1. Fixed dose regime
  2. Symptomatic / triggered regime
  3. Loading dose regime
Fixed dose: first calculate no. of units = % × volume(mL) / 1000. If daily units is 30 → chlordiazepoxide 30 mg qds + PRN 30 mg.
  • Moderate dependence — taper off over 5–7 days
  • Severe dependence — taper off over 7–10 days
  • If chlordiazepoxide contraindicated → can give lorazepam. 1 mg lorazepam = 25 mg chlordiazepoxide

Management of Withdrawal Seizures

  1. Acute management of seizure — suck out secretions. Put in lateral position, O₂.
  2. BZD or Carbamazepine — loading dose. After initial management, exclude other causes of seizures.
  3. History of withdrawal seizure / seizure during current episode → seizure prophylaxis (BZD or carbamazepine)
  4. The chlordiazepoxide dose

Mx of Withdrawal Seizures

  • Doc — BZD
  • If patient not already on BZD, start chlordiazepoxide
  • If patient already on chlordiazepoxide, optimise the dose
  • Repeat basic Ix, take neurology opinion if suspecting other cause, other than withdrawal seizures
  • No need for prophylactic anti-epileptics

Alcohol → Sudden Stop → Hyperexcitability

  • Alcohol enhances GABA activity & ↓ glutamate activity
  • About 45 minutes – 1 hour time for alcohol to reach the brain

Long-term Management Goals

  • Maximising motivation for abstinence
  • Help to reconstruct life w/o alcohol
  • Relapse prevention

05 · Prophylaxis & Rx for Wernicke's

  • Oral thiamine → mild–moderate withdrawal & no Wernicke — 200 mg/day
  • IV / IM → Wernicke or delirium — 200–300 mg/day

Thiamine & Chlordiazepoxide (CDPX) — Detailed

Thiamine

  1. Community setting — oral 200–300 mg/day (daily or bd)
  2. Severe dependence with malnutrition → IM/IV thiamine 300 mg daily × 5 days, then convert to oral 300 mg daily
  3. Suspected or confirmed Wernicke's encephalopathy → Delirium tremens
    • IV 500 mg TDS for 5 days & then
    • IV 300 mg daily for 5 days
    • Then convert to oral thiamine

Thiamine Regime (liver stores only adequate for 1–2 hrs)

  • Mild–moderate withdrawal symptoms → oral thiamine 200–300 mg/day during detoxification, for 3–5 days, convert to oral 100 mg daily; community setting 100 mg daily
  • Delirium tremens — IM/IV 200–300 mg/day × 5 days, followed by oral 300 mg daily
Wernicke's Encephalopathy
IV Thiamine 400–600 mg tds × 5 days
Followed by 200–300 mg/day × 5 days
(Ophthalmoplegia resolves first, then oral)

Wernicke–Korsakoff Syndrome

Acute thiamine reduction — brain stem acute demyelination.

  • Wernicke's (reversible) — Ophthalmoplegia / Ataxia / Confusion
    • Encephalopathy — disorientation / indifference / inattentiveness
    • Oculomotor dysfunction — nystagmus / lateral rectus palsy / conjugate gaze palsy
    • Ataxia — gait
  • Korsakoff's (irreversible) — Antegrade & retrograde amnesia / poor recall / impairment of recent memory / disorientation / poor insight

Alcohol Withdrawal — Staged Progression

StageTimingFeatures
Stage IW/T 8 hrsHeadache, GI discomfort (N/V/D), tremors, insomnia, palpitations
Stage IIW/T 12–24 hrs↑PR & BP, rapid breathing, convulsion
Stage IIIW/T 24–92 hrsHallucinations, seizures, delirium tremens — severe withdrawal

80% have peripheral neuropathy.

In alcoholic hallucinosis, patient is NOT confused (distinguishes from delirium tremens).

Delirium Tremens

Confusion / Agitation / Visual hallucination / Tactile hallucination — fever, tachycardia, ↑BP. Occurs in 24–72 hrs of stopping alcohol/cocaine tremors.

Delirium Tremens — Management

  • Management in HDU or medical unit
  • Management in calm, safe environment where excessive light, sound stimulation is minimised
  • Adequate nutrition & hydration
  • Monitor BP / PR / RR
  • Ix → LFT to differentiate delirium due to liver failure or delirium tremens
Titrating dose regime of BZD — loading dose & titrate 2-hourly according to severity of symptoms. Total dose in 1st 24 hrs given as divided doses; dose can be reduced by day 2 if no complications.

High-dose BZD Regime for Delirium Tremens (CCD2P)

High dose CCD2P — 5–40 mg every 2–3 hourly

Impaired liver function → short acting BZD (Clorazepam / Oxazepam).

  • In 2–3 days: taper. Ix — FBC, LFT, U&E, Ca balance, seizures
  • Examine for hypoglycaemia / TIA / meningism

Seizure Management

  1. Acute mx of seizure — suck out secretions, put in lateral position, O₂
  2. BZD or carbamazepine — loading dose, after initial mx exclude other causes of seizures
  3. Hx of withdrawal seizure / seizure during current episode → seizure prophylaxis (BZD or carbamazepine)
Withdrawal seizures — within 24–48 hrs of stopping alcohol.

Pharmacological Management — Relapse Prevention

1. Acamprosate

↓ Craving (maintains abstinence). Given for 6 months or more.

2. Disulfiram

  • Inhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase → accumulates acetaldehyde
  • If alcohol consumed while on disulfiram → unpleasant & dangerous: throbbing headache / flushing / nausea / vomiting / sweating / palpitations / arrhythmia / ↓BP
  • Not a craving reducer — it's a deterrent
  • For 6–8 months
  • Side effects — drowsy / ↓libido / metallic taste in mouth / breath odour

3. Naltrexone

Relapse prevention.

4. Topiramate

Relapse prevention.

More effective when combined with psychological therapy — motivational enhancement & MI.

Withdrawal Symptoms

Heroin — short half-life → develops withdrawal symptoms several times/day. Withdrawal symptoms occur within 6–24 hrs, lasts for 5–7 days.

  • Lacrimation
  • Rhinorrhea
  • Increased sweating
  • Piloerection
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Insomnia
  • Hot & cold flushes
  • Body aches / muscle cramps
  • Anxiety
  • Restlessness
  • ↑PR / ↑BP / ↑T
  • Yawning
  • Pupillary dilatation

Mild Withdrawal Management

Rx of mild withdrawal — 2–3 L of water/day. Vit B/C supplements.

Symptomatic Rx: reduce sweating / diarrhoea / vomiting / abdominal cramps / anxiety.

Moderate to severe: + clonidine or opioid medication (Buprenorphine / Methadone / Codeine phosphate).

Consequences of Heroin Dependence

1. Medical

  • Poor nutrition
  • IV users → Hepatitis B/C, HIV, local abscess & ulcers
  • Respiratory depression due to accidental overdose

2. Social & Financial

  • Illegal smuggling or selling, crime & commercial sex work to earn money
  • Forensic involvement — arrest / imprisonment
  • Income / job / relationship (family) issues

3. Psychiatric

  • Depression / suicidal attempts / ADHD / antisocial personality disorder
Wernicke–Korsakoff — encephalopathy (disorientation/indifference/inattentiveness); oculomotor dysfunction (nystagmus/lateral rectus palsy/conjugate gaze palsy); ataxia.
Opioids — μ receptors: ↓GI motility → constipation, respiratory depression, ↓cough reflex.

Management of Heroin Dependence

1. Engaging the Patient

Regular & frequent contact with the patient.

2. Deciding the Goals of Rx

  • Main goal — abstinence
  • Intermediate — engage with Rx programme / discourage risky behaviour, minimise financial impact, encourage occupation & healthy relationships
  • Harm reduction — ↓ needle sharing / methadone maintenance programme

3. Deciding Rx Setting

No need for in-patient care (heroin withdrawal not life threatening). Can admit to ↓ access.

4. Detoxification of Dependent Patients / Withdrawal Mx

  • Reassurance
  • BZD — for sleep & anxiety
  • NSAIDs — for body aches
  • Opioid agonists — Methadone / Buprenorphine (detox in IV users)
  • Loperamide for diarrhoea (running nose, gooseflesh)
  • (Methadone — maintenance therapy)

5. Enhancing Motivation

  1. Identify stage of motivation (Prochaska & DiClemente cycle of change)
  2. In pre-contemplation → general information about heroin & its harm
  3. In contemplation → consider pros & cons of drug use
  4. In action stage → advice on how to cut down or stop drug

6. Coping with High Risk Situations

  • Strategies to minimise contact with dealers
  • Encourage dealers to take up another occupation
  • Address the heroin-using subculture
  • Use community-based Rx services

7. Optimising Quality of Life

  • Relationships / family life / occupation / health / leisure activities / imprisonments
  • Encourage refrain from illegal activities
  • Better financial management
  • ↓ money on heroin / ↑ in good AOL (activities of living)
  • Family therapy

Support & Advice

  • Support without discrimination & stigmatisation. Don't reject or blame the person.
  • Advice on harm reduction strategies (availability of Rx / not to take other prescribed meds etc.)
  • Clearly point out the physical, psychological & social problems caused by drugs
  • Make future assessments for STD services / immunisation etc.
  • Discuss strategies to avoid or cope with high risk situations
  • Make specific plans to avoid drug use
  • Identify family or friends who will support stopping drug use
  • Patients who do not succeed or relapse — identify & give credit for any success. Discuss situations which led to relapse.
