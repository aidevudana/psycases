PDD 01

  1. Must be present for at least 3 months / single or set of related delusions.
  2. Clearly personal rather than sub cultural.
  3. Depressive episodes may present intermittently, but delusion persist at times when there is no mood disturbance.
  4. No evidence of brain dx / No hx of schizophrenia symptoms / No or only occasional auditory hallucinations.
  5. Absence of depressive (manic or mixed episode.
  6. persistent?
  7. symptoms are not a manifestation of other medical condition

BAD 03

At least 2 mood episodes — at least 1 mania or hypomania with one episode of major depression.

★ BAD; current episode manic w/o psychotic symptoms.

  1. Should last for at least 1 week.
  2. Severe enough to disrupt ordinary work & social activities.
  3. Elated mood
  4. Increased energy.
  5. Pressure of speech / decreased need for sleep / grandiosity / excessive optimism.

★ BAD; current episode mania with psychotic symptoms.

  1. Inflated self esteem and grandiose ideas → develop into delusions
  2. Irritability & suspiciousness.
  3. Persecutory delusions.
  4. Grandiose or religious delusions of identity
  5. Speech is incomprehensible due to flight of ideas & pressure of speech.
  6. Aggression or violence.
  7. Neglect of eating, drinking & personal hygiene. → dehydration & self neglect.

Schizophrenia 04

  1. Any one of (a) to (d) or
  2. Any two of (e) to (h)
  3. Present for 1 month.
Commonest subtypes: ★ Paranoid schizophrenia  ·  ★ Hebephrenic  ·  ★ Catatonic  ·  ★ Undifferentiated
  1. Thought echo, thought insertion, withdrawal, thought broadcasting.
  2. Delusion of control, Delusional perception
  3. Hallucinatory voices giving running commentary. Discussing the pt. among themselves (3rd person)
  4. Persistent delusion of other kinds that are culturally inappropriate and completely impossible. (Religious or political identity or superman powers)
  5. Persistent hallucination in any modality persistent, overvalued ideas
  6. Incoherent or irrelevant speech; Neologism
  7. Catatonic behavior — excitement, posturing, waxy flexibility, negativism, mutism, stupor.
  8. Negative symptoms — apathy, paucity of speech, incongruent emotional response, social withdrawal.
  9. Significant change in overall quality of life.

Side notes on figure: Anhedonia · (drive) · Alogia (poverty of speech) · Avolition (impaired motivation & drive) · Affective flattening · Attective Platterning · Modality

Schizoaffective disorder 03

★ Schizoaffective dx; Manic type. (↑ energy (overactivity / impaired concentration))

  1. Prominent elevation of mood / Less obvious elevation of mood,
  2. Combined with increased irritability or excitement.
  3. At least one or preferably two, typically schizophrenic symptoms (a-d)

★ Schizoaffective dx; Depressive type.

  1. Prominent depression — with 2 characteristic depressive symptoms or associated behavioral abnormalities.
  2. Within some episode, at least one, preferrably two typical schizophrenic symptoms (a-d).

Depression 06 2 weeks ⊕

  1. Depressed mood
  2. Loss of interest & enjoyment.
  3. Reduced energy, ↑ fatiguability.

Other

  1. Reduced concentration & attention.
  2. Reduced self esteem & self confidence.
  3. Ideas of guilt & unworthiness.
  4. (Bleak) & pessimistic views of the future
  5. Ideas or acts of self harm or suicide.
  6. Disturbed sleep
  7. Diminished appetite
Somatic features: Psychomotor retard · LOA · Low · Sleep disturbance

Severity grades

Mild

with somatic Xn → 2 typical ⊕ 2 other
w/o somatic Xn → 2 typical ⊕ 2 other

Moderate

with somatic Xn → 2 typical ⊕ 3/4 other, 4≤ somatic symptoms ⊕
w/o somatic Xn → 2 typical ⊕ 3/4 other

Severe with psychotic symptoms

3 typical ⊕ 4 other ⊕ delusion, hallucination, depressive stupor.

Severe w/o psychotic symptoms

3 typical ⊕ 4 other. Auditory or olfactory hallucinations.
Recurrent depression — 2 or more episodes, lasts for 2 wks, b/w 3-12 months interval, w/o significant mood disturbances.

Dementia 07

cognitive domains · praxis / visuoperception

  1. Decline in both memory & thinking. (Washing / dressing / eating / personal hygiene / excretory or toilet activities).
  2. Impair personal activities of daily living.
  3. Memory impairment typically affect registration, storage & retrieval of new information.
  4. Impairment of thinking & reasoning capacity.
  5. Reduction in the flow of ideas.
  6. Processing of incoming information impaired.
  7. Difficult to attend to more than one stimulus at a time.
  8. Consciousness intact.
  9. Symptoms should be there for at least 6 months
  10. 2 of 6 cognitive domains should be affected*.

★ Alzheimer's dementia (AD) — Dementia in Alzheimer's dx.

  1. Presence of dementia.
  2. Insidious onset with slow deterioration
  3. No evidence to suggest other systemic or brain disease which can induce dementia (Hypothyroidism / Hypercalcemia / B12 deficiency)
  4. Absence of sudden focal neurological features — Hemiparesis, sensory loss, visual field defects.
Dementia in AD with early onset. · Dementia in AD with late onset.

Dependence (Alcohol) 08

Dependence:

  1. Strong desire to take the substance.
  2. Difficulty in controlling substance taking behavior.
  3. Physiological withdrawal state when substance use has stopped / reduced.
  4. Evidence of tolerance — increased dose of the substance is required.
  5. Progressive neglect of alternative pleasure because of the substance use.
  6. Persistent with substance use despite clear evidence of harmful consequences.
★ Should have 3 or more present together at some time during the previous year.

Alcohol dependence

  • ↳ Currently abstinent
  • ↳ Currently abstinent, but in a protected environment.
  • ↳ Currently on a clinically supervised maintenance or replacement regimen.
  • ↳ Currently abstinent but receiving treatment with aversive or blocking agents.
  • ↳ Comorbid with

Withdrawal state ↳ Uncomplicated  ·  ↳ Complicated.

OCD 09

Must present on most days for at least 2 wks & interfere with activities (Distress cause affect to ADLs)

  1. They are individual's own thoughts or impulses.
  2. There must be at least one thought or act that is still resisted unsuccessfully.
  3. Thought of carrying out act must not itself be pleasurable.
  4. Thoughts, images or impulses must be unpleasantly repetitive.

Agoraphobia 10

↳ without panic dx.    ↳ with panic dx.

① Primary anxiety symptoms.
② Restricted to particular situation (at least 2).
③ Avoidance behavior.

All 3 needed:

  1. Symptoms must be 1° manifestation of anxiety, not 2° to other symptoms such as delusions or obsessional thoughts.
  2. Anxiety must be restricted to at least 2 of the following situations — crowds / public places / travelling away from home / travelling alone. → significant distress caused by avoidance.
  3. Avoidance of phobic situation.
  4. At least one symptom of autonomic arousal plus one other anxiety symptom in the feared situation.

Social phobia / Specific phobia 11

  1. Symptoms must be primary manifestations of anxiety & not 2° to other symptoms such as delusions or obsessional thoughts.
  2. Anxiety must be restricted to / predominate in particular social situations / objects.
  3. Phobic situation / object is avoided — due to marked fear of being the focus of attention.
  4. 2 symptoms of anxiety ⊕ one of → shaking / fear of vomiting / fear or urgency of micturition or defecation.
  5. Significant emotional distress.
DSM-5: 6 months

Panic dx 12

  1. Several severe attacks of autonomic anxiety should have occurred within a period of 1 month.
  2. Occurred in circumstances where there is no objective danger.
  3. Occur without being confined to known or predictable situations. (unpredictable)
  4. Comparative freedom from anxiety symptoms between attacks.
Note: see criteria above ★

Somatoform dx / Somatisation disorder 13

Should present all,

  1. At least 2 years of multiple & variable physical symptoms without adequate physical explanation.
  2. Persistent refusal to accept the advice or reassurance of several doctors, that there is no physical explanation for the symptoms.
  3. Some degree of impairment of social & family functioning

Generalized anxiety dx 14

  • Symptoms of anxiety must be present for most day for at least several weeks at a time & usually for several months
  • Worries about future misfortune, difficulty in concentration (Apprehension).
  • Motor tension (Restless fidgeting / tension headache / trembling / inability to relax).
  • Autonomic overactivity (lightheadedness, sweating, tachycardia & tachypnea / epigastric discomfort / dizziness / dry mouth).
  • Primary symptoms of anxiety. Should not fulfil the criteria for mood disorder or other anxiety disorder.

Adjustment dx 15

  • Onset usually within 1 month of the occurrence of stressful event.
  • Duration of symptoms does not usually exceed 6 months
  • Interfere with social functioning & performance.
  • Pc: depression, anxiety, worry, feeling of inability to cope, disability in performing daily routine.

PTSD 03

  1. Occur within 6 months of a traumatic event of exceptional severity.
  2. Repetitive, intrusive recollection of memory, dreams.
  3. Avoidance of reminders.
  4. Emotional detachment & numbness of feeling.
  5. Autonomic disturbances, mood disorder, behavioral abnormalities.

PTSD — cont. Diagnostic criteria — Lec Note

  1. Exposure to a traumatic event.
  2. Persistence of intrusion symptoms following a traumatic event.
  3. Persistence avoidance.
  4. Negative alteration of cognition & mood at traumatic event.
  5. Marked alteration of arousal & reactivity at traumatic event.
  6. Duration of disturbance > 1 month.
  7. Significant distress & impairment.
  8. Disturbance not attributable to physiological effect of substance.

New lec note:

Criteria A — Post traumatic event. (PTE).

Criteria B — Recurrent memories of PTE.

  • Involuntary, intrusive, distressing memories.
  • Distressing dreams of PTE.
  • Flashbacks
  • Dissociative symptoms — Altered sense of reality
  • Intense & prolonged psychological distress
  • Psychological reaction.

Criteria C → Active avoidance.

Criteria D — Negative alteration of cognition & mood.

  • Inability to recall important aspects of PTE
  • Negative belief & expectation about the world.
  • Distorted cognition of cause & consequences
  • Diminished interest.
  • Feeling detached from other people.
  • Inability to experience ⊕ emotions

Criteria E — Irritable & anger outburst. Reckless self destructive behavior.
